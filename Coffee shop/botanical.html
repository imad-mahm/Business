Folio I · an apothecary that serves coffee

An hour,
a cup,
a season.

Verdant Hours is a small, slow coffee bar at the back of the herbalist's. Six seats, one window, a kettle that has not stopped in three years.

We serve coffee by the cup & by the season. We do not hurry the brew, and we do not hurry you.

Salma Ren, proprietress

No. IV

— From a notebook kept behind the counter

Field notes.

To wait for a slow filter is to refuse the speed of every other thing. The water knows it is being poured slowly. The grounds know. The kettle knows. We have nothing else to do for these six minutes & this is not nothing.

When I opened Verdant Hours I was told the menu was too short, the bar too small, the hours too odd. The herbalist who shares the building told me the opposite. She said: fewer things, done better, by a smaller number of people, more slowly. She had a name for this. She called it apothecary's time.

Apothecary's time is not nostalgic. It is just careful. It pays attention to the body in front of it & the cup in front of that body. It does not need to be photographed. It does not need to be optimised. It needs to be drunk, slowly, while the light moves across the table.

We are open six days a week, between mid-morning & mid-afternoon. The garden is open all year. If you come, plan to stay for a while. We will pour you something, and then we will pour you something else.

Salma Ren

Proprietress & brewer
