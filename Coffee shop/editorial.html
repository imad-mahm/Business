IV

A short letter from the back room

How we got here.

I spent eight years behind other people's bars before I opened this one. Most of those years I was somewhere else in my head, thinking about the shop I'd build if I ever stopped saying yes to shifts. Halfway is that shop. It's small on purpose. The menu is short on purpose. The hours are odd on purpose, because the best cups happen at 10 a.m. and 3 p.m., and the rest of the day belongs to the kitchen.

We buy green coffee from three importers we've worked with for years. We roast on a 1-kilo drum on Mondays, rest the beans for at least a week, and pull the last bag before the next roast lands. Nothing sits long. Nothing has to.

If you've never had filter coffee that tastes like fruit, ask. If you've had a hundred and want to argue about Geshas, ask louder. The stools are comfortable. The light is good in the morning.

Maya Okafor

Owner & head roaster
