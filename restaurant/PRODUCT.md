# PRODUCT.md — SORREL (demo) / ATELIER restaurant identity studio

## Register
brand

## Product Purpose
This is a sales demo built by **ATELIER**, a studio that builds websites for
independent shops and restaurants. The demo shows a single fictional restaurant,
**SORREL**, rendered in five distinct signature design directions a prospective
owner can switch between to choose the identity they want. The goal is to prove
ATELIER produces authored, characterful sites, not generic template output.

SORREL is a modern neighborhood bistro: seasonal small plates, natural wine,
warm but design-forward, broadly relatable so any owner can imagine their place
in its shoes.

## Users
- **Primary:** an independent restaurant owner evaluating ATELIER. Not technical.
  Judges on taste, warmth, and "does this feel like *my* place."
- **Secondary:** the diner who would actually use the live site to browse the
  menu and reserve a table or place an order.

## The signature move (shared across all five directions)
**The Service Clock.** Every ATELIER site is alive. A time-aware element reads the
visitor's local clock and surfaces: the current day-part (Brunch / Lunch / Dinner /
Late / Closed), whether the kitchen is open right now, and a countdown to the next
service change. The page's accent warmth shifts subtly by time of day. This is the
recognizable thread that says "an ATELIER site is alive" and the core
differentiator versus static template makers. Each direction expresses it in its
own visual language; the logic is shared, the styling is not.

## Brand / Tone
Confident, warm, crafted. Each direction has its own voice but none is corporate,
none is "SaaS-cream," none leans on the category reflex (no generic
"rustic-wood-and-chalkboard" restaurant cliché).

## Anti-references
- Generic restaurant templates: chalkboard fonts, stock-photo hero, centered
  everything, "Welcome to our restaurant."
- Wix/Squarespace default look.
- The hero-metric SaaS template.
- Identical card grids of dishes.

## Strategic principles
- Five directions must be *strongly* differentiated in palette, type, layout, and
  mood. If two could be confused, push them apart.
- The Service Clock must be present and functional in all five.
- Each direction is its own self-contained world (its own file, its own CSS). No
  shared visual template, so nothing feels mass-produced.
- Reservations/orders is a real, usable dummy flow in each direction, not a
  placeholder link.
